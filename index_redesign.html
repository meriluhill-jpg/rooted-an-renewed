Your Story Matters

For the Parent at the Window

Nobody tells you what to do with your hands when your child stops calling. There are no casseroles for this grief. No cards at the Hallmark store. No language in most churches for the particular ache of loving someone who has chosen distance.

If you are holding this book, you probably know that ache. You have probably tried more than people realize. Prayer. Counsel. Conversations in your head that never happened. Books, podcasts, online groups, late-night searches.

I know because I am living it.

Before He restored a relationship — He wanted to restore my heart.

This study is not a formula for getting your prodigal back. There is no guaranteed outcome. But there is an invitation. To bring your whole heart before God. To stop performing strength when you are tired. To let Him restore what has been worn down by waiting.

God sees you. Not just your situation — you.

The mother who has replayed a thousand conversations. The one who still checks her phone. The one who wonders what she did wrong. The one who wants to hope but is afraid hope will hurt too much.

He sees the nights you cannot sleep. The mornings you pretend to be okay. The moments you catch yourself reaching for your phone to call someone who will not call back.

And in the middle of all that unseen ache, He is doing something sacred. He is restoring your soul. Building your faith on something stronger than outcomes. Rebuilding trust in Him that runs deeper than the distance between you and your child.

This study walks through twelve weeks of Scripture, reflection, and prayer designed specifically for the parent in waiting. For the one who needs God to restore them first.

He restores my soul. Not someday. Not if the relationship is repaired. Now. In this waiting. In this ache.

About Merilu

The Author & Teacher

"He restores my soul."

Merilu Hill is a writer, podcaster, and community leader based in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Her work weaves together faith, family, and a passion for helping people live with courage and clarity.

She is the author of Your Mindset Matters, The Sparkle Journal, Girl, Be Fearless, and several other books exploring personal growth and faith-centered living. She hosts the weekly podcast Rooted and Renewed, where she shares stories of struggle and resilience and interviews voices shaping conversations about faith, confidence, and personal transformation.

With over twenty years of experience in counseling, coaching, and community leadership, Merilu brings deep insight into how people navigate change, fear, and the search for meaning. Her work is grounded in Scripture and shaped by real life — the kind of faith that shows up on ordinary Tuesdays and holds steady through extraordinary pain.

She lives in Beaver, Pennsylvania with her husband Ken, their four children, and two Boston Terriers. When she's not writing or recording, you'll find her reading, making coffee that's too strong, or standing at a window thinking about grace.
